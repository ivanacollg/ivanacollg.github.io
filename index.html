Ivana Collado Gonzalez

Marine robotics - Perception - Autonomy

Ph.D. Candidate | Mechanical Engineering - Robotics

About · Research · Publications · CV

My research focuses on robot perception, navigation, and decision-making for autonomous systems operating in complex marine settings. I am experienced in multi-sensor fusion, planning under uncertainty, and experimental validation in challenging underwater environments.

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Stevens Institute of Technology. I am advised by Dr. Bendan Englot and part of the Robust Field Autonomy Lab (RFAL). I hold a Masters degree in Robotics Engineering from Stevens Insitute of Technology, and a Bachelors degree in Mechatronics Engineering from Tecnológico de Monterrey (ITESM), Mexico.

By developing algorithms that perform reliably in real-world conditions, I aim to enable fully autonomous systems for underwater infrastructure maintenance and environmental monitoring. I am passionate about advancing technologies that promote sustainability and strengthen ecological integrity.

Research Interests: Marine robotics, perception, mapping, SLAM, exploration, planning under uncertainty, decision-making, and multi-sensor fusion.
